4. Origami: The Intersection of Art, Math, and Engineering

Origami, evolving since the Heian period (794–1185),
is the art of creating complex forms from a single sheet of paper — without cutting or gluing.

Today:

  • NASA uses the Miura-Ori fold to deploy solar panels.
  • MIT develops Nanoscale Origami to fold DNA.
  • Robert Lang creates lifelike insects using computational origami.

This discipline is the universal language of foldability —
and in VOart, it is expressed through 117 unique folding techniques.

7. VOart Rarity System: Rarity 2.0

7.1 Objective Difficulty Scoring (TDC)

Each folding technique is evaluated across three dimensions:

DimensionWeightDescription
Physical Execution40%Motor skill required for hand-folding
Conceptual Depth30%Mathematical/theoretical understanding
Design Complexity30%Systemic depth of the technique

Final Score = (Physical × 0.4) + (Conceptual × 0.3) + (Design × 0.3)
Transparent, repeatable, community-verifiable

7.2 Weighted Rarity Formula

Rarity Score = (TDC × 0.45) + (AS × 0.25) + (PP × 0.20) + (CV × 0.10)
  • Lower score = rarer
  • TDC (Folding Technique): 1–10
  • AS (Aura): 1–10
  • PP (Paper): 1–10
  • CV (Color): 1–10
